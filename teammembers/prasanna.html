Q: What’s the difference between merging and rebasing in Git?

A: Merging combines two branches with a merge commit, preserving history, while rebasing rewrites history by applying commits on top of another branch for a cleaner, linear history.

"Tidy First?" by Kent Beck presents a practical approach to improving software design. Instead of jumping into new features, Beck encourages making small, thoughtful cleanups—like renaming variables, breaking down large functions, or reorganizing code structure—right before you work on a section. These low-risk improvements don’t aim for perfection, but rather make the next step easier and safer. He emphasizes that good design evolves gradually, driven by real feedback. The key message: keep it simple, stay flexible, and improve as you go.
